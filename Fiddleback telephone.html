Fiddleback telephone

Fiddleback telephones take their name from the shape of the telephone which is reminisent of the shape of a fiddle. This phone is more compact than the more commonly seen type 85 phone which has the bells facing forward. The type 294, along with its cousin, the type 85 are common battery phones, meaning the power was supplied by an external source rather than internal batteries and a magneto. The manufacture date of this particular telephone is written inside as 1912.
